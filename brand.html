Gulgulo Brand System · v1
Gulgulo

A Konkani word for the handmade terracotta water pot — clay body, clay lid — that keeps water cool and gives it a soft, mineral taste. The still point a small family of products traces back to. Grounded, intimate, unhurried.

01 The Mark Single-weight outline · the bare pot leads; the lid is held in reserve

A rounded, holdable belly; a short, gently narrowed neck; a softly flared lip. The primary mark is the bare pot — quiet and open. A domed clay lid with a small knob is kept as a reserve variant for when the full gulgulo needs naming.

The only ornament is the fine throwing-rings incised on the belly as the pot turns on the wheel. The whole mark is one even weight so it reads as a quiet, drawn line — never an illustration.

It must hold at a footer mark or favicon. The simplified, single-ring cut is reserved for those tiny sizes.
